Steward Agreement
Last updated: 2026-03-02
The oath
I will review only what I’m qualified to review. I will act in good faith. I won’t trade approvals for money, favors, or attention. I will write clear notes that explain my decision. I will respect the person behind the proof — even when I deny.
1) Competence + scope
  • You will only accept reviews you can evaluate fairly and competently.
  • If you can’t judge the evidence, you will decline (or request reassignment if available).
  • You will not “guess” approvals or denials when the proof is unclear.
2) Good faith review
  • Review the claim and the proof — not the submitter’s personality, politics, or status.
  • Be consistent: apply the same standard to everyone.
  • Explain your reasoning in calm, factual notes.
3) Conflicts of interest
  • Do not review submissions from friends, family, close associates, competitors, or anyone where you have direct benefit.
  • If you realize a conflict after starting, stop and disclose it to Proofworks.
  • “I can be fair anyway” is not the standard — the record must be clean.
4) No bribery, pressure, or pay-to-approve
  • You will not accept bribes, gifts, favors, discounts, or off-platform compensation from submitters.
  • You will not threaten submitters, solicit payments, or imply approvals are for sale.
  • Any attempt to buy your decision should be reported to admin@proofworks.us.
5) Privacy + confidentiality
  • Submissions often contain private information. Treat all proof as confidential unless the submitter makes it public.
  • Do not download, repost, share, or discuss private proof outside Proofworks.
  • Use the minimum necessary detail in notes. Avoid doxxing or unnecessary personal information.
6) Professional conduct
  • No harassment, discrimination, or retaliation.
  • No profanity-laced notes. Keep the record readable for third parties.
  • Be direct, not cruel.
7) Audit trail
Your notes are part of the permanent record. Write so an honest stranger can understand why the decision happened.
8) Compensation (if offered)
Steward compensation may be offered for qualified reviews. It is never payment for an outcome. Policies may change over time.
9) Suspension or removal
Proofworks may suspend or remove steward privileges at any time, with or without notice. Stewardship is a privilege, not a right.
10) Independent role
Stewards act as independent reviewers. This is not employment. Nothing here creates a partnership, agency, or fiduciary duty.
